BIHAR'S FOOD



Bihari cuisine is a flavorful and diverse blend of vegetarian and non-vegetarian dishes, rooted in the agrarian culture of Bihar and influenced by seasonal produce and traditional cooking methods. It is known for dishes like Litti Chokha—roasted wheat flour balls stuffed with spiced sattu (roasted gram flour) served with mashed vegetables—Sattu Paratha, Dal Pitha (rice dumplings), and spicy mutton curry. Mustard oil, panch phoron (a five-spice blend), and smoked flavors are commonly used, giving the food a bold and rustic character. Sweets like Thekua, Khaja, and Malpua also hold a special place in Bihari festivals and daily life, showcasing the region’s love for simple yet satisfying fare.

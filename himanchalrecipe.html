Chana Madra

Chana Madra is a creamy curry made with chickpeas and yogurt, flavored with mild spices. Soak 1 cup of kabuli chana (white chickpeas) overnight, then boil until soft. In a pan, heat mustard oil, add cumin seeds, cinnamon, cloves, and bay leaf. Add 1 cup thick whisked yogurt slowly, stirring constantly on low heat to prevent curdling. Once the yogurt thickens and starts releasing oil, add boiled chickpeas and salt. Cook the mixture on low flame, adding garam masala, turmeric, and coriander powder. Let it simmer until the gravy becomes thick and creamy. Garnish with chopped coriander and serve hot with steamed rice or roti. Chana Madra is a staple of Himachali dham (feast) and showcases the state’s love for yogurt-based gravies.



Babru

Babru is a Himachali stuffed flatbread similar to a kachori. Soak black urad dal overnight and grind to a coarse paste with ginger, green chili, and salt. For the dough, knead wheat flour with a little oil and water until soft. Roll out small dough balls, fill them with the urad dal mixture, seal and flatten them gently. Deep fry until golden brown and crispy on both sides. Babru is typically served with tamarind chutney or curd and makes a hearty breakfast or snack. The earthy taste of urad dal inside a crispy shell makes it a beloved comfort food across Himachal.



Tudkiya Bhath

Tudkiya Bhath is a spiced rice dish cooked with lentils and potatoes. Soak 1 cup rice and ½ cup masoor dal. In a pressure cooker, heat ghee and sauté cumin seeds, cloves, bay leaves, cinnamon, and chopped onions. Add chopped tomatoes, ginger, garlic, and green chilies, followed by diced potatoes. Add turmeric, salt, and a pinch of garam masala. Drain rice and dal, add to the cooker with enough water, and pressure cook until everything is soft and fragrant. Finish with lemon juice and fresh coriander. Tudkiya Bhath is often eaten with curd or pickle and is the Himachali version of comfort rice, mildly spiced and deeply nourishing.



Kullu Siddu

Siddu is a steamed stuffed bread, popular in the Kullu region. For the dough, mix wheat flour with water and yeast, knead it, and let it rest for a few hours to rise. For stuffing, roast poppy seeds and walnuts, grind them coarsely, and mix with green chili, salt, and a little ghee. Divide the dough into balls, stuff with this mixture, and seal them. Steam the stuffed dough balls in a steamer or over boiling water until firm and cooked through. Siddu is usually served hot with ghee, dal, or chutney. Its warm, nutty filling and fluffy texture make it a favorite during winters



Bhey

Bhey is a traditional Himachali dish made from lotus stem. Peel and slice the lotus stem into thin rounds, boil until tender. In a pan, heat mustard oil, add cumin seeds, ginger-garlic paste, and chopped onions. Once golden, add turmeric, red chili powder, coriander powder, and chopped tomatoes. Cook till the tomatoes break down. Add the boiled lotus stem slices and sauté until they absorb the masala. Add a little water and simmer for 10–15 minutes. Garnish with fresh coriander. Bhey is unique to Himachal cuisine and is enjoyed with chapatis or parathas for a satisfying mountain meal.



Sepu Vadi

Sepu Vadi is a lentil-based curry made from ground urad dal dumplings. Soak urad dal overnight, grind into a thick paste, and mix with spices. Shape small dumplings and steam or deep-fry them until golden. For the curry, sauté onions, tomatoes, ginger, and garlic in mustard oil. Add turmeric, coriander, and fennel powder. Add curd slowly to form a thick gravy, then add the fried vadis and simmer for 10–15 minutes. The vadis soak up the flavorful yogurt gravy, making Sepu Vadi a hearty main dish. It’s traditionally served during Himachali dham with rice or roti.
